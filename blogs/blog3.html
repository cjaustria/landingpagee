The Man Who Sits with Everybody

Leadership, soft skills, and career vision for tech professionals.

“People don’t buy what you do, they buy why you do it.”
Simon Sinek

Have you ever wondered why some people naturally inspire others, even when others have more technical skill or experience? Soft skills are often overlooked in tech. Most people focus on hard skills. Para sa akin, leadership is about connection. Minsan, kahit gaano ka kagaling, walang mangyayari kung hindi ka marunong makipag-ugnayan. The mediocre person with strong soft skills often has more leverage than the genius who doesn’t talk to anybody.

I’ve always been inspired by fictional leaders: Aragorn, Optimus Prime, Batman. Leadership is not about authority alone—it’s about presence, influence, and understanding people.

Books like Leaders Eat Last, Atomic Habits, and The 7 Habits of Highly Effective People taught me that habits, purpose, and people-first leadership build long-term impact. This illustrates the Kaizen principle—small, consistent improvements lead to massive change over time.

The title The Man Who Sits with Everybody reflects my philosophy: a true leader does not isolate themselves at the top but sits among the team, listens, and understands.

In conclusion, I seek leadership because it challenges and transforms. Technical skills open doors, but connection and soft skills sustain the journey. My vision: to learn, to lead, to build influence, and eventually guide teams and organizations with empathy, strategy, and purpose.
