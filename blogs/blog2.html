The Way of Machiavelli

Reflections on leadership, influence, and strategy from a lifelong student leader.

Intro Body Conclusion

Introduction

“It is better to be feared than loved, if you cannot be both.”
Niccolò Machiavelli

Ang kapangyarihan ay hindi laging tungkol sa pagiging mabait o popular. Growing up, I never planned to be a leader. Leadership chose me. In school, in organizations, even in small groups of friends, I realized that people looked to me for direction, for decisions, for guidance.

Body

This unexpected role brought tension. My personal belief in doing good and being ethical, inspired by Stoic teachings of Epictetus, often collided with the realities of power. The lessons of Stoicism and Machiavelli taught me that leadership requires both moral awareness and practical strategy.

  • Democratic Leadership: Collaboration and consensus-driven decision making.
  • Authoritarian Leadership: Quick, precise decisions in high-pressure situations.
  • Supportive Leadership: Guidance, motivation, and building confidence in teams.

In my experience, leadership requires balancing morality and strategy. Stoicism guided my ethical choices; Machiavelli taught practical navigation through real-world challenges.

Conclusion

Ultimately, a true leader is measured not by followers, but by the courage to make difficult decisions and the wisdom to use power responsibly.
